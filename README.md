# Informational Health Auditor

**Informational Health Auditor** is an algorithmic auditing tool designed to detect and diagnose the structural collapse (heat death) of data caused by "social desirability bias" (peer pressure and conformity) hidden in large-scale survey and evaluation data ($N \ge 2000$).

## 📖 Background

In the era of big data, the assumption that "increasing the sample size $N$ brings us closer to the truth" does not hold in systems where social biases exist. From the perspectives of statistical mechanics and information geometry, this tool retrospectively detects the **"nonlinear evaporation of warning signals (minority voices)—the Information Cliff"** that occurs when peer pressure exceeds a certain critical point. In doing so, it debugs the self-contradictions introduced by standard data science preprocessing (such as outlier removal).

> **Reference:** Kawahata, Y. (2026). *Proposing 'Informational Health Diagnostics' in Computational Social Science: Phase Transitions of Minority Signals Induced by Peer Pressure and the Epistemological Limits of Big Data.* Applied Sciences (Submitted).

## ✨ Core Features

This tool automatically executes three "informational health recovery prescriptions" on pre-aggregated CSV data.

1. **Audit of Rejected Anomalies**
* Scores whether the data routinely discarded as "outliers ($3\sigma$)" in standard machine learning pipelines actually contains high-confidence (high $\beta$) genuine SOS signals.


2. **Bayesian Stress Test**
* Infers the true distribution by assuming the observed, statistically significant distribution is a "false image generated by conformity." It calculates the **KL divergence** against the observed distribution to warn against overconfidence in p-values.


3. **Small-Data Ensemble Audit**
* Intentionally divides large-scale data into minimal subgroups ($N \le 50$) to measure the "variance (chaos)" between groups. It visualizes the proportion of the space where "heat death due to conformity (zero variance)" is occurring.
